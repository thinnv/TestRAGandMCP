1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE
This MSA establishes the general terms under which Service Provider will deliver cloud infrastructure and IT services to Client. Specific projects defined in individual Statements of Work (SOW).

2. SERVICES COVERED
Service Provider may provide:
- Cloud infrastructure hosting and management
- 24/7 monitoring and support
- Database administration and optimization
- Security and compliance services
- Disaster recovery and backup
- Network management
- Software deployment and updates
- Technical consulting

3. TERM AND TERMINATION
Effective Date: May 1, 2025
Initial Term: 3 years
Auto-Renewal: Annual unless terminated with 90 days notice
Termination for Convenience: 180 days written notice
Termination for Cause: 30 days to cure breach

4. SERVICE LEVELS AND PERFORMANCE
Uptime Guarantee: 99.9% monthly uptime
Response Times:
- Critical (P1): 15 minutes
- High (P2): 2 hours
- Medium (P3): 8 hours
- Low (P4): 24 hours

Service Credits:
- 99.0-99.9% uptime: 10% monthly credit
- 95.0-99.0% uptime: 25% monthly credit
- Below 95.0% uptime: 50% monthly credit

5. PRICING STRUCTURE
Base Infrastructure Fee: $25,000/month
Includes:
- 50TB storage
- 500GB bandwidth
- 10 virtual servers
- Basic monitoring and support

Additional Services (per SOW):
- Additional storage: $0.15/GB/month
- Additional bandwidth: $0.10/GB
- Additional servers: $500/server/month
- Premium support: $5,000/month
- Consulting: $200/hour

Annual Adjustment: Lesser of 3% or CPI increase

6. PAYMENT TERMS
Billing Cycle: Monthly in advance
Payment Due: Net 30 from invoice date
Late Fees: 1.5% per month on overdue amounts
SOW Payments: As specified in individual SOWs
Disputed Charges: Must notify within 15 days

7. DATA SECURITY AND COMPLIANCE
Service Provider Shall:
- Maintain SOC 2 Type II certification
- Comply with HIPAA regulations
- Encrypt data at rest and in transit
- Perform annual security audits
- Maintain cybersecurity insurance ($5M)
- Conduct background checks on staff
- Provide security incident reports within 24 hours

8. CONFIDENTIALITY
Both parties agree to:
- Protect confidential information
- Use information only for Agreement purposes
- Return or destroy information upon termination
- Maintain confidentiality for 5 years post-termination

Protected Information Includes:
- Technical specifications and architecture
- Patient health information (PHI)
- Business strategies and pricing
- Security procedures

9. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY
Pre-existing IP: Remains with respective owner
Developed IP: Client owns custom developments
License: Service Provider grants Client license to use platform
Third-party Software: Licensed separately as needed

10. WARRANTIES
Service Provider Warrants:
- Services performed professionally
- Compliance with applicable laws
- No infringement of third-party rights
- Personnel properly trained and qualified
- Disaster recovery procedures tested quarterly

11. LIABILITY AND INDEMNIFICATION
Service Provider Liability Cap: 12 months of fees paid
Exceptions to Cap:
- Willful misconduct
- Gross negligence
- Breach of confidentiality
- Security breaches due to Provider negligence

Indemnification:
Each party indemnifies other for their own:
- Negligence or willful misconduct
- Breach of Agreement
- Infringement of IP rights
- Violation of laws or regulations

12. DISASTER RECOVERY AND BUSINESS CONTINUITY
Recovery Time Objective (RTO): 4 hours
Recovery Point Objective (RPO): 1 hour
Backup Frequency: Continuous with hourly snapshots
Backup Retention: 30 days standard, 7 years for compliance
DR Testing: Quarterly with annual full test
Geographic Redundancy: Data replicated to secondary region

13. INSURANCE REQUIREMENTS
Service Provider Maintains:
- General Liability: $5M
- Professional Liability: $5M
- Cyber Liability: $10M
- Workers Compensation: Statutory limits

14. AUDIT RIGHTS
Client may audit Service Provider annually with 30 days notice for:
- Security compliance
- Financial records related to services
- Performance metrics
- Data handling procedures

15. CHANGE MANAGEMENT
Changes to production environment require:
- Change request submitted 5 business days prior
- Client approval for changes affecting availability
- Rollback plan for all changes
- Communication plan for user-impacting changes

16. DISPUTE RESOLUTION
Escalation Process:
1. Technical teams attempt resolution (5 days)
2. Management escalation (10 days)
3. Executive escalation (10 days)
4. Mediation (30 days)
5. Binding arbitration in Houston, TX

17. FORCE MAJEURE
Neither party liable for delays due to events beyond reasonable control, including natural disasters, war, terrorism, or government actions.

18. GOVERNING LAW

This MSA and all executed SOWs constitute entire agreement between parties.
