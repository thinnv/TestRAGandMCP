WHEREAS, the parties wish to explore a potential business relationship regarding blockchain-based payment security solutions ("Purpose"); and

WHEREAS, each party may disclose Confidential Information to the other in connection with such discussions;

NOW THEREFORE, the parties agree as follows:

1. DEFINITION OF CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION

1.1 "Confidential Information" means any information disclosed by one party ("Disclosing Party") to the other party ("Receiving Party"), whether orally, in writing, or by inspection of tangible objects, including:

(a) Technical Information: Source code, algorithms, software architecture, API specifications, system designs, technical documentation, product roadmaps, research and development plans, prototypes, and technical specifications.

(b) Business Information: Customer lists, supplier information, pricing strategies, marketing plans, business strategies, financial information, sales data, forecasts, market analyses, and competitive analyses.

(c) Strategic Information: Merger and acquisition plans, partnership strategies, expansion plans, organizational structures, and business methods.

(d) Proprietary Information: Trade secrets, know-how, inventions (whether patentable or not), discoveries, improvements, and any other proprietary information.

1.2 Information marked as "Confidential," "Proprietary," or with similar designation shall be considered Confidential Information.

1.3 Information disclosed orally shall be confirmed in writing within 30 days to be considered Confidential Information.

2. EXCLUSIONS FROM CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION

Confidential Information shall not include information that:

(a) Was publicly known at the time of disclosure or becomes publicly known through no breach of this Agreement by Receiving Party;

(b) Was rightfully known by Receiving Party prior to disclosure by Disclosing Party, as evidenced by written records;

(c) Is rightfully received by Receiving Party from a third party without breach of any confidentiality obligation;

(d) Is independently developed by Receiving Party without use of or reference to Disclosing Party's Confidential Information, as evidenced by written records; or

(e) Is approved for release by written authorization of Disclosing Party.

3. OBLIGATIONS OF RECEIVING PARTY

3.1 Non-Use: Receiving Party shall use Confidential Information solely for the Purpose and not for any other purpose without prior written consent of Disclosing Party.

3.2 Non-Disclosure: Receiving Party shall not disclose Confidential Information to any third party except as permitted under Section 4.

3.3 Protection: Receiving Party shall protect Confidential Information using at least the same degree of care it uses to protect its own confidential information, but in no event less than reasonable care.

3.4 Access Limitation: Receiving Party shall limit access to Confidential Information to employees, contractors, and advisors who:
- Have a legitimate need to know for the Purpose;
- Have been informed of the confidential nature of such information; and
- Are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as protective as this Agreement.

3.5 Notification: Receiving Party shall promptly notify Disclosing Party of any unauthorized use or disclosure of Confidential Information.

4. PERMITTED DISCLOSURES

4.1 Legal Requirement: If Receiving Party is required by law, court order, or government regulation to disclose Confidential Information, it shall:
- Provide Disclosing Party with prompt written notice;
- Cooperate with Disclosing Party's efforts to seek protective order;
- Disclose only the minimum information required; and
- Use reasonable efforts to obtain confidential treatment.

4.2 Professional Advisors: Receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information to its attorneys, accountants, and other professional advisors who are bound by professional confidentiality obligations.

5. OWNERSHIP AND RETURN OF MATERIALS

5.1 All Confidential Information remains the property of Disclosing Party.

5.2 No license or right is granted under any patent, copyright, trade secret, or other intellectual property right except the limited right to use for the Purpose.

5.3 Upon termination of this Agreement or upon request by Disclosing Party, Receiving Party shall:
- Promptly return all tangible materials containing Confidential Information;
- Delete or destroy all electronic copies; and
- Certify in writing completion of such return/destruction.

5.4 Receiving Party may retain one copy of Confidential Information solely for legal compliance purposes, subject to continuing obligations of this Agreement.

6. NO OBLIGATION TO DISCLOSE

Neither party is obligated to disclose any Confidential Information. Each party may determine in its sole discretion what information, if any, to disclose.

7. NO OBLIGATION TO PROCEED

This Agreement does not create any obligation for either party to proceed with any transaction or business relationship. Either party may terminate discussions at any time without liability.

8. NO WARRANTY

ALL CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION IS PROVIDED "AS IS" WITHOUT WARRANTY OF ANY KIND. Neither party makes any representation regarding accuracy or completeness of its Confidential Information.

9. TERM AND TERMINATION

9.1 Term: This Agreement commences on the date first written above and continues for 2 years, unless earlier terminated.

9.2 Termination: Either party may terminate this Agreement at any time with 30 days written notice.

9.3 Survival: Obligations regarding Confidential Information disclosed prior to termination shall survive termination for 5 years.

10. REMEDIES

10.1 Breach of this Agreement may cause irreparable harm for which monetary damages may be inadequate.

10.2 Disclosing Party shall be entitled to seek equitable relief, including injunction and specific performance, in addition to all other remedies available at law or equity.

10.3 Receiving Party shall be liable for breaches by its employees, contractors, and agents.

11. EXPORT COMPLIANCE

Each party agrees to comply with all applicable export control laws and regulations. Neither party shall export or re-export Confidential Information in violation of such laws.

12. NO REVERSE ENGINEERING

Receiving Party shall not reverse engineer, disassemble, or decompile any prototypes, software, or other tangible objects disclosed by Disclosing Party.

13. GENERAL PROVISIONS

13.1 Governing Law: This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of Delaware, without regard to conflicts of law principles.

13.2 Jurisdiction: Any disputes shall be resolved exclusively in state or federal courts located in Delaware.

13.3 Entire Agreement: This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement regarding confidentiality and supersedes all prior agreements.

13.4 Amendment: This Agreement may be amended only by written instrument signed by both parties.

13.5 Waiver: No waiver of any provision shall be effective unless in writing. Waiver of one breach shall not constitute waiver of any other breach.

13.6 Severability: If any provision is held invalid or unenforceable, remaining provisions shall continue in full force.

13.7 Assignment: This Agreement may not be assigned without prior written consent of the other party, except to successor in connection with merger or acquisition.

13.8 Notices: All notices shall be in writing and delivered by email (with confirmation), courier, or certified mail to addresses above.

13.9 Counterparts: This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, each deemed an original.
