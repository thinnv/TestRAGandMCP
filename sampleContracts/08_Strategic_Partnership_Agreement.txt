STRATEGIC PARTNERSHIP AGREEMENT

This Strategic Partnership Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of June 1, 2025, by and between:

PARTY A: GreenEnergy Solutions Corporation
Address: 1500 Renewable Way, Denver, CO 80202
Represented by: Amanda Foster, Chief Executive Officer
Email: a.foster@greenenergysolutions.com
("GreenEnergy")

PARTY B: SmartGrid Technologies Inc.
Address: 2200 Innovation Parkway, Austin, TX 78701
Represented by: James Park, President
Email: j.park@smartgridtech.com
("SmartGrid")

Collectively referred to as "Partners" or individually as "Partner."

RECITALS

WHEREAS, GreenEnergy is a leading provider of residential and commercial solar panel systems with over 50,000 installations across North America;

WHEREAS, SmartGrid develops advanced energy management systems and IoT devices for optimizing renewable energy consumption and storage;

WHEREAS, the Partners desire to collaborate to offer integrated solar-plus-storage solutions with intelligent energy management to customers;

WHEREAS, this partnership will combine GreenEnergy's solar installation expertise with SmartGrid's energy management technology to create superior customer value;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants and promises herein, the Partners agree as follows:

1. PARTNERSHIP PURPOSE AND OBJECTIVES

1.1 Joint Mission: Accelerate adoption of integrated renewable energy and intelligent energy management systems for residential and commercial customers.

1.2 Specific Objectives:
(a) Develop bundled solar-plus-storage packages with SmartGrid's energy management system
(b) Create joint go-to-market strategy targeting existing and new customers
(c) Establish integrated sales and installation processes
(d) Develop co-branded marketing materials and campaigns
(e) Share customer insights and market intelligence
(f) Pursue joint bids for large commercial and utility-scale projects
(g) Collaborate on product development and feature roadmaps

1.3 Target Metrics (Year 1):
- 5,000 integrated system installations
- $75 million combined revenue from partnership
- 90% customer satisfaction rating
- 15% market share in smart solar segment

2. SCOPE OF COLLABORATION

2.1 Product Integration:
- SmartGrid to develop solar inverter integration with its energy management platform
- GreenEnergy to certify SmartGrid devices for all solar installations
- Joint testing and quality assurance protocols
- Unified mobile app experience for customers
- Interoperable warranty and support systems

2.2 Sales and Marketing:
- Joint sales training for both companies' teams
- Co-branded marketing campaigns (60/40 cost split: GreenEnergy/SmartGrid)
- Shared trade show presence at 6+ industry events annually
- Cross-referrals of leads and opportunities
- Joint customer case studies and testimonials
- Coordinated social media and content marketing

2.3 Technical Collaboration:
- Quarterly product roadmap alignment meetings
- Shared customer feedback loops
- Joint participation in industry standards bodies
- Collaborative R&D on next-generation features
- Integration support and technical documentation

3. ROLES AND RESPONSIBILITIES

3.1 GreenEnergy Responsibilities:
- Solar panel procurement, installation, and commissioning
- Permitting and interconnection processes
- Electrical inspection coordination
- Primary customer relationship for solar systems
- Installation of SmartGrid devices during solar deployment
- First-line customer support for solar equipment
- Warranty service for solar components
- Customer financing options and programs

3.2 SmartGrid Responsibilities:
- Energy management hardware and software
- Mobile app and cloud platform
- Integration with utility demand response programs
- Software updates and feature enhancements
- Second-line support for energy management issues
- Data analytics and energy optimization algorithms
- Battery storage system integration
- API access for GreenEnergy systems

3.3 Joint Responsibilities:
- Integrated system design and sizing
- Customer education and training
- Quality assurance and testing
- Marketing collateral development
- Sales process and pricing structure
- Customer support escalation procedures
- Performance monitoring and reporting

4. FINANCIAL TERMS

4.1 Revenue Sharing Model:
For bundled installations:
- GreenEnergy receives: 65% of total contract value (solar equipment and installation)
- SmartGrid receives: 35% of total contract value (energy management system)

4.2 Referral Fees:
- GreenEnergy pays SmartGrid 12% for customer referrals (solar-only installations)
- SmartGrid pays GreenEnergy 12% for customer referrals (retrofit energy management)

4.3 Support Cost Sharing:
- Each Partner bears own costs for first-line support
- Joint escalation support costs split 50/50
- Warranty claims handled by respective manufacturers

4.4 Marketing Investment:
Year 1 Joint Marketing Budget: $3,000,000
- GreenEnergy contribution: $1,800,000 (60%)
- SmartGrid contribution: $1,200,000 (40%)
- Quarterly budget reviews and adjustments
- Marketing committee approves expenditures over $50,000

4.5 Volume Incentives:
Quarterly installation targets with bonus structure:
- 1,000-1,499 units: 2% bonus to both Partners
- 1,500-1,999 units: 4% bonus to both Partners
- 2,000+ units: 6% bonus to both Partners
Bonuses paid quarterly based on achieved volumes.

5. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

5.1 Pre-existing IP: Each Partner retains ownership of its pre-existing intellectual property.

5.2 Joint IP: Any intellectual property created jointly shall be co-owned by both Partners with equal rights to use, license, and commercialize.

5.3 Licensing:
- GreenEnergy grants SmartGrid non-exclusive license to use its branding in joint materials
- SmartGrid grants GreenEnergy non-exclusive license to use its software and APIs for integrated installations
- No licensing fees for partnership-related uses

5.4 Improvements: Improvements to each Partner's existing IP remain the property of that Partner.

5.5 Trademarks: Neither Partner may register trademarks confusingly similar to the other's marks.

6. CONFIDENTIALITY AND DATA SHARING

6.1 Confidential Information:
Both Partners agree to protect each other's confidential information including:
- Proprietary technology and trade secrets
- Customer lists and contact information
- Pricing strategies and cost structures
- Business plans and strategies
- Financial information
- Product roadmaps

6.2 Standard of Care: Reasonable care, but no less than protection afforded to own confidential information.

6.3 Customer Data:
- Joint customers: Both Partners have access to relevant customer data
- Data use limited to partnership purposes and legal compliance
- Compliance with all privacy laws (GDPR, CCPA, etc.)
- Shared data security protocols and incident response
- Customer consent obtained for data sharing

6.4 Survival: Confidentiality obligations survive termination for 5 years.

7. GOVERNANCE

7.1 Partnership Steering Committee:
Composition: 3 representatives from each Partner
Responsibilities:
- Strategic direction and goal setting
- Budget approval and resource allocation
- Dispute resolution
- Performance review
- Major partnership decisions

Meetings: Quarterly (minimum), with special meetings as needed
Decision Making: Consensus preferred; escalation process for deadlocks

7.2 Operational Working Groups:
- Sales and Marketing Committee
- Product Integration Committee
- Customer Success Committee
- Finance Committee

7.3 Executive Sponsors:
GreenEnergy: Amanda Foster, CEO
SmartGrid: James Park, President
Escalation point for unresolved issues

8. TERM AND TERMINATION

8.1 Initial Term: 3 years from June 1, 2025

8.2 Renewal: Automatic renewal for successive 1-year terms unless either party provides 180 days written notice of non-renewal.

8.3 Termination for Convenience: Either party may terminate with 180 days written notice after initial term.

8.4 Termination for Cause: Either party may terminate immediately if:
- Material breach uncured after 60 days written notice
- Bankruptcy, insolvency, or receivership
- Violation of exclusivity provisions (if applicable)
- Breach of confidentiality
- Fraud or misrepresentation

8.5 Wind-Down Period: 90-day wind-down to complete pending customer commitments.

8.6 Effect of Termination:
- Fulfill existing customer obligations
- Cease new joint sales efforts
- Continue support for installed base (minimum 2 years)
- Return or destroy confidential information
- Final financial settlement within 60 days

9. EXCLUSIVITY AND NON-COMPETITION

9.1 Non-Exclusive Partnership: This is a non-exclusive partnership. Each Partner may:
- Pursue other partnerships in complementary areas
- Develop competitive products
- Serve customers independently

9.2 Competitive Activity Notification: Partners agree to notify each other of strategic partnerships with direct competitors at least 30 days in advance.

9.3 Customer Protection: Neither Partner shall solicit the other's employees or contractors during the term and for 1 year following termination.

10. WARRANTIES AND REPRESENTATIONS

10.1 Each Partner Warrants:
- Legal authority to enter this Agreement
- No conflicting agreements or obligations
- Ownership or right to use all contributed IP
- Compliance with all applicable laws and regulations
- No pending or threatened litigation affecting ability to perform

10.2 Product Warranties:
- Each Partner provides standard warranties for its products
- Warranty terms clearly communicated to customers
- Warranty service obligations defined in operations manual

11. LIABILITY AND INDEMNIFICATION

11.1 Liability Limitation: Except for breaches of confidentiality or IP infringement, neither Partner shall be liable for indirect, consequential, or punitive damages.

11.2 Liability Cap: Total liability for any claim shall not exceed the fees received by the liable party in the 12 months preceding the claim.

11.3 Mutual Indemnification: Each Partner shall indemnify the other against third-party claims arising from:
- Its products or services
- Its negligence or willful misconduct
- Breach of this Agreement
- Violation of laws or regulations
- Infringement of third-party IP rights

11.4 Insurance: Each Partner shall maintain:
- Commercial General Liability: $5,000,000
- Professional Liability: $5,000,000
- Product Liability: $5,000,000
- Cyber Liability: $3,000,000

12. PERFORMANCE METRICS AND REPORTING

12.1 Key Performance Indicators:
- Monthly installation volumes
- Customer satisfaction scores (NPS)
- Revenue generated through partnership
- Lead conversion rates
- Support ticket resolution times
- Product defect rates
- Customer retention rates

12.2 Reporting: Monthly dashboard shared by 10th of following month.

12.3 Quarterly Business Reviews: In-depth performance analysis and strategy adjustments.

13. GENERAL PROVISIONS

13.1 Governing Law: State of Colorado

13.2 Dispute Resolution:
- Good faith negotiation (30 days)
- Mediation (30 days)
- Binding arbitration under AAA rules

13.3 Entire Agreement: This Agreement supersedes all prior agreements and understandings.

13.4 Amendment: Must be in writing signed by authorized representatives of both Partners.

13.5 Force Majeure: Neither party liable for delays due to events beyond reasonable control.

13.6 Assignment: Neither party may assign without prior written consent, except to successor entity in merger or acquisition.

13.7 Notices: Written notices to addresses above, effective upon email confirmation or 3 days after mailing.

13.8 Independent Contractors: Partners are independent contractors. This Agreement does not create joint venture, partnership, or agency relationship.
