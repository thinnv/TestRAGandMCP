1. SERVICES TO BE PROVIDED

3. COMPENSATION STRUCTURE
Monthly Retainer: $15,000
Hourly Rate (beyond retainer): $350/hour
Travel Expenses: Reimbursed at cost with receipts
Maximum Monthly Cap: $25,000

Total Annual Value: Approximately $180,000 - $300,000

4. PAYMENT TERMS
- Retainer paid first business day of each month
- Additional hours billed monthly with 15-day payment terms
- Late payments subject to 1.5% monthly interest
- Expenses submitted with supporting documentation

5. CONSULTANT OBLIGATIONS
Consultant shall:
- Dedicate minimum 40 hours per month to Client
- Maintain professional liability insurance ($2M coverage)
- Provide monthly progress reports
- Be available for emergency consultations (48-hour response)
- Maintain strict confidentiality of client information

6. CLIENT RESPONSIBILITIES
Client agrees to:
- Provide access to necessary personnel and data
- Respond to consultant requests within 3 business days
- Designate primary point of contact
- Provide workspace during on-site visits
- Cover travel costs for required site visits

7. DELIVERABLES
- Monthly status reports
- Quarterly strategic recommendations
- Annual comprehensive business review
- Process improvement documentation
- Training materials for staff

8. INDEPENDENT CONTRACTOR STATUS
Consultant is an independent contractor, not an employee. Consultant responsible for own taxes, insurance, and benefits.

9. NON-COMPETE CLAUSE
During engagement and for 12 months after, Consultant agrees not to provide similar services to direct competitors identified in Schedule A.

10. CONFIDENTIALITY AND NON-DISCLOSURE
Both parties agree to protect confidential information with same care as own proprietary information. Obligation survives termination for 5 years.

11. TERMINATION
- Either party may terminate with 60 days written notice
- Immediate termination for breach of confidentiality
- Client pays for services rendered through termination date
- Return of all confidential materials within 10 days

12. INDEMNIFICATION
Each party indemnifies the other against claims arising from their own negligence or misconduct.

13. DISPUTE RESOLUTION
Disputes shall first go to mediation. If unsuccessful, binding arbitration in New York City under AAA rules.
